Other Questions You May Have…
How much of the recruitment process does FindEd help with?

After sending your vacancy to the right candidates, you’ll receive applications and you can choose to

  • Invite them for an interview
  • Shortlist for further review
  • Politely decline

After you’ve invited them for interview, we’ll check out whether they’re happy to go ahead and then send their contact details to you via email to arrange the next stage.

What about equal opportunities and references?

We collect that information for you too. You receive it in separate documents once the candidate has confirmed they would like to meet you for interview.

What about anti canvassing and relations at the school policies?

We haven’t included this in the process because it would make the one click application impossible. If you need this information we suggest asking it whilst arranging the interview.

What about background checks?

We ask the questions about criminal record and barred lists. If there is a criminal record we ask the candidate to send you details in an envelope marked for the head teacher. If the candidate confirms that they’re on the barred list we remove them from the system in accordance with the law. The school is responsible for carrying out DBS checks as you usually would with candidates before employing them.
